Apply for employment funding from the UK Shared Prosperity Fund

The employment portion of the UK Shared Prosperity Fund provides funding to organisations to provide tailored support to help people who struggle to access mainstream employment provision and to support them move closer to, or into, employment.

How the Fund works

The Fund covers all areas of the UK. Each area has its own invitation that lists the needs and priorities being addressed in that area. These have been identified by local organisations.

Organisations can apply for funding through an invitation. They need to deliver a project that helps to address some of the needs and priorities listed in the invitation.

Who can apply

Any organisation able to deliver a project that can meet some of the needs and priorities that are being addressed in the area they are applying for.

You must have a UK bank account.

How to apply

You can apply for funding through an invitation. This states the different needs and priorities being addressed in that area. Your project must respond to some of the needs and priorities listed in the invitation. There are invitations for each area of the UK.

Find an invitation for UKSPF on GOV.UK.

When do you need to apply by

Each invitation will have a deadline. You will need to check the invitation you are applying for.

What you need to provide

You must tell us:

  • the reference number of the invitation you are applying for
  • your Companies House reference number, Charity Commission number or Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator number, if you have one or another accredited body that you are registered with
  • how much you are applying for
  • the needs and priorities from the invitation you are addressing in your application
  • what you will use the money for
  • how you will manage the money
  • how you will manage the performance of your project
  • your organisation name and address
  • your application contact details
  • your organisation bank account details

DWP performs due diligence checks on all organisations using the Cabinet Office Spotlight tool.
