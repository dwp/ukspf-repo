Apply for local employment and skills funding

If you're an organisation looking to support people facing multiple or complex needs across the UK, you can apply for a grant from the UK Shared Prosperity Fund.

How the fund works

The funding is split between different areas of the UK. You can apply for funding for your area through an invitation. The invitation details the criteria that has been set for each area. You can apply for one invitation.

How to apply

You can apply for a grant from the UK Shared Prosperity Fund by:

  • applying online yourself
  • applying through another organisation by partnering with them

What is an invitation?

Each area of the UK has an invitation that states what the local needs are being addressed in that area for the UK Shared Prosperity Fund (Employment).

The invitations have been written through discussions with local authorities. These discussions have highlighted what needs should be addressed in that area.

What you'll get

You'll get money to support a project that supports the needs of your local area. How much money you can apply for depends on the amount outlined in the invitation for your local area.

Criteria for the Fund

What you need to provide

You will need:

  • your Companies House reference number, Charity Commission number or Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator number, if you have one
  • your organisation address and contact details
  • details of your project and how it addresses the criteria

DWP performs due diligence checks on all organisations using the Cabinet Office Spotlight tool.
